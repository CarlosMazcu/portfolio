01. About Me

Hello! I'm Carlos Mazcuñán Blanes, a game programmer from Spain currently pursuing an HND in Videogame Programming at ESAT. I specialize in developing video games and interactive simulations with a strong focus on performance and technical excellence.

My background in hospitality honed my teamwork and problem-solving skills, particularly under pressure. As a Higher Technician in Automotive Engineering, I gained valuable experience during a 3-month internship with a competitive team, where I learned the importance of precision and meticulous attention to detail in complex systems.

My technical skill set includes proficiency in C, C++, and ARM Assembler with some Python experience. I'm comfortable working with Unity and Unreal Engine 5, and I'm well-versed in version control systems like Git and Perforce. I've developed expertise in debugging and optimizing code for real-time applications.

During my studies, I've worked on two significant projects. The first was a video game developed collaboratively with artists, programmers, and designers, giving me experience in teamwork across disciplines and implementing game mechanics.

The second project involved building a graphics engine from scratch in C++ using OpenGL. Working closely with a teammate, I implemented core features including rendering, shading, and texture mapping, deepening my understanding of low-level programming and game engine architecture.

I thrive in collaborative environments and have experience working with diverse teams including engineers and designers, which has enhanced my communication skills and adaptability.
